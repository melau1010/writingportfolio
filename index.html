The Future of Banking: KYC, GDPR, & Blockchain

Digitized banking via blockchain technology has permeated underbanked regions, ushering in a new era in digitized money transactions and record-keeping. In response, banks have expanded internationally in order to develop enterprise-level global banking infrastructure. With numerous financial enterprises attempting to expand without considering new privacy laws and technology protocols, central points of failure and information security breaches are at risk of multiplying. Policy & Procedure Manual

Policies and procedures provide structure, safety, risk management, and clarity for a company's executives, management, and staff. The USA PATRIOT Act of 2001 created requirements for all money services businesses (MSBs) to develop anti-money laundering (AML) departments and policies. Section 326 of the PATRIOT ACT's Customer Identification Program emphasizes the importance of Knowing Your Customer (KYC). KYC procedures have continued to expand internationally and digitally at the cryptocurrency, blockchain level. Click here to see a comprehensive AML policy for a cryptocurrency start-up.
